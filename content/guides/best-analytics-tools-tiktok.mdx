## TikTok native analytics

TikTok's built-in analytics dashboard is accessed from your Profile → Creator tools → Analytics. It shows you views, followers, profile visits, engagement rate, video performance breakdowns, and — importantly for Creator Rewards creators — qualified view data.

For most creators, this is enough. The data is sourced directly from TikTok (obviously), it updates within 24–48 hours, and it shows you the metrics that actually affect Creator Rewards earnings: qualified views, completion rate, and audience geography. The geography data matters because Creator Rewards RPM varies by region — knowing where your views come from is directly relevant to your payout.

The limitation is historical range. TikTok's native analytics shows approximately 60 days of data for most metrics [UNVERIFIED — TikTok has adjusted data windows; verify current limits in Creator Studio]. If you want to track longer-term trends or compare year-over-year performance, you need a third-party tool that stores your data.

Start here. Don't pay for a third-party tool until you've used native analytics consistently for at least 30 days and identified a specific question it can't answer.

## Exolyt

Exolyt is a TikTok-specific analytics platform — not a general social media tool. That focus means it does TikTok analytics in more depth than multi-platform tools that add TikTok support as an afterthought.

The most useful feature for independent creators is competitor tracking. You can monitor other accounts' growth, video performance, and posting cadence. For creators trying to understand what's working in their niche — without spending hours manually watching competitor content — this is a genuine time saver.

Exolyt also tracks hashtag performance, which helps with content planning if your strategy involves trending or niche hashtags.

## Analisa.io

Analisa.io provides audience demographic breakdowns and longer historical data windows than TikTok's native analytics. The demographic data — age, gender, location, interests — is particularly useful for creators working with brand partnerships, since brands often ask for audience demographics before agreeing to deals.

The free tier has meaningful limitations; the paid tier is where the deeper data lives. Pricing at Analisa.io [UNVERIFIED — verify current plan structure].

Worth it if: you're pitching brand deals and need audience data to share with brands, or if you want longer-term trend data for content strategy.

Not worth it if: you're focused purely on Creator Rewards optimization and aren't doing brand deals yet.

## Pentos

Pentos focuses on hashtag and sound analytics — tracking performance of specific hashtags and audio tracks on TikTok. This is useful for creators whose content strategy involves trending sounds or niche hashtag communities.

If your content doesn't lean heavily on trending audio or hashtag discovery, Pentos solves a problem you probably don't have yet. For creators who do build their strategy around sound and hashtag trends, the data depth is more specific than you'll get from general analytics tools.

No confirmed public affiliate program [UNVERIFIED]. Current pricing at pentos.io.

## Later

Later is primarily a scheduling tool that includes TikTok analytics as part of its platform. If you need both scheduling and analytics — and many creators do — it's worth considering as a combined solution rather than paying for two separate tools.

The analytics aren't as TikTok-specific as Exolyt or Pentos, but they're solid for tracking posting performance, identifying best-posting-time patterns, and managing a multi-platform presence alongside TikTok.

Later offers a free plan with limited features. Paid plans include more analytics depth and scheduling features — current pricing at later.com [UNVERIFIED].

## Sprout Social

Sprout Social is enterprise-grade social media management software. It's comprehensive, well-built, and priced for marketing teams and agencies — not individual creators. Most TikTok creators don't need it, and the price makes it hard to justify for a solo creator on a content budget.

If you're managing TikTok for multiple clients, a brand account, or you're part of a team with multiple people accessing the same analytics data, Sprout Social is worth evaluating. For everyone else, the tools above will serve you better at a fraction of the cost.

## What to track (regardless of which tool you use)

The metrics that matter most for Creator Rewards specifically:

- **Qualified views:** Not total views — qualified views. This is what determines your payout.
- **Completion rate:** Higher completion rate correlates with more qualified views. Your target: understand which video lengths and formats drive the highest completion rate in your content.
- **Audience geography:** RPM varies significantly by region. If a large portion of your audience is in regions with lower RPM rates, that affects your earnings regardless of total view count.
- **Follower growth trend:** Relevant for staying above the 10,000 follower threshold, and for brand deal conversations.

<CalloutBox type="tip">
  TikTok's native analytics shows qualified view data directly. Start there before paying for third-party tools. The analytics gap is about depth and history — not about missing the basics.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does TikTok show qualified views in its native analytics?**
Yes. Creator Rewards analytics within TikTok's Creator Studio shows qualified view data separately from total views. You don't need a third-party tool to see this [UNVERIFIED — TikTok's analytics interface changes periodically; verify current layout in Creator Studio].

**What's the difference between views and qualified views?**
Total views count every time someone's video plays. Qualified views count only views that meet TikTok's criteria — including minimum video length (1 minute+), minimum watch time, and eligible region. Your Creator Rewards payout is based on qualified views, not total views.

**How often does TikTok analytics update?**
Approximately every 24–48 hours for most metrics [UNVERIFIED — TikTok's update cadence may vary].

**Do I need analytics tools to apply for Creator Rewards?**
No. The application process only requires meeting eligibility thresholds (followers, views, account type, region). Analytics tools help you understand your performance after you're approved — they're not part of the application.
