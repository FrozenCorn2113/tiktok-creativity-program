# TikTok Creator Rewards vs YouTube Shorts: Which Pays More?

Both TikTok and YouTube pay creators, but they reward different content styles and audience behaviors. This guide compares eligibility, payout models, and who should prioritize each platform in 2026.

## Eligibility comparison

TikTok Creator Rewards:
- 10K followers  
- 100K views in 30 days  
- Personal account  
- 1+ minute content  

YouTube Shorts monetization:
- 1,000 subscribers + 10M Shorts views (90 days)  
- Or full YouTube Partner thresholds  

## Payout model differences

TikTok pays **RPM per 1,000 qualified views**. YouTube Shorts pays **ad revenue share** from a pooled fund.

That means:
- TikTok rewards **retention and niche clarity**.  
- YouTube rewards **volume and consistency**.

## Who should prioritize TikTok?

Choose TikTok if:

- You can create 1+ minute content consistently  
- You have a clear niche and expertise  
- You’re in an eligible country  

## Who should prioritize YouTube Shorts?

Choose YouTube if:

- You want broader global reach  
- You already run a YouTube channel  
- You can produce high volume
