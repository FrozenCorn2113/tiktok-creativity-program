# Why Is My TikTok RPM Dropping? How to Fix It

If your RPM suddenly drops, you’re not alone. TikTok’s Creator Rewards payouts can fluctuate, but big drops usually have **specific causes**. This guide explains the most common reasons and how to stabilize your RPM.

<CalloutBox type="info" title="Quick facts">
  <ul>
    <li>RPM is tied to qualified views, completion rate, and viewer region.</li>
    <li>Small changes in audience mix can cut payouts by 30–50%.</li>
    <li>Consistency matters more than one-off viral spikes.</li>
  </ul>
</CalloutBox>

## 1. Your audience shifted to lower-RPM countries

This is the #1 reason for sudden drops. If a larger percentage of views come from non-eligible or lower-RPM countries, your payout falls.

**Fix:** Compare the last 7 days to the previous 30 days in analytics. If the mix shifted, adjust targeting and content language.

## 2. Completion rate fell

TikTok rewards videos that keep people watching. If your hooks slip, RPM slides.

**Fix:** Shorten intros, tighten pacing, and cut 3–5 seconds of fluff per video.

## 3. More views are unqualified

Qualified views are only For You Page views on eligible 1+ minute content posted after approval.

**Fix:** Audit your last 10 videos for length, format, and original content.

## 4. Engagement slowed

Fewer comments, saves, and shares typically reduce RPM.

**Fix:** Ask one clear question per video to spark comments and saves.

## 5. Content is too broad

Generic content gets views but doesn’t earn high RPM consistently.

**Fix:** Narrow your niche and repeat the same format for two weeks.

## 6. Posting gaps

Long gaps can reset momentum.

**Fix:** Post at least 3–4 times per week for 30 days.

## 7. Reused or recycled content

TikTok prioritizes original edits and clear value.

**Fix:** Add new context, voiceover, or format before reposting.

## 8. Platform testing periods

Sometimes TikTok tests your content with different audiences.

**Fix:** Focus on consistency and wait 7–10 days before making big changes.
