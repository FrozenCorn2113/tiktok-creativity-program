# How to Appeal TikTok Creator Rewards Rejection

Getting rejected from Creator Rewards is frustrating — but most rejections are fixable. This guide covers the most common reasons, the appeal process, and what to change before reapplying.

<CalloutBox type="info" title="Quick facts">
  <ul>
    <li>Most rejections are due to account type, eligibility timing, or content format.</li>
    <li>You can appeal inside TikTok or reapply after eligibility resets.</li>
    <li>Clear documentation increases approval chances.</li>
  </ul>
</CalloutBox>

## Common rejection reasons

1. **Account type is Business**  
2. **Not enough followers or recent views**  
3. **Recent content under 60 seconds**  
4. **Community guideline warnings**  
5. **Non-eligible country signals**

If you’re unsure which applies, check your creator tools and the last 30 days of content.

## The appeal process (step-by-step)

1. Open TikTok → Profile → Creator tools → Creator Rewards.  
2. Select the rejection notice.  
3. Tap **Appeal** and submit your explanation.  
4. Provide supporting details (dates, follower count, eligible content list).

## What to include in your appeal

- Confirmation of **personal account**  
- Proof of **10K+ followers**  
- Screenshots of 1+ minute content posted after eligibility  
- Confirmation of eligible country

<CalloutBox type="tip">
  Be concise and factual. Appeals that read like a checklist perform best.
</CalloutBox>

## When to reapply instead of appealing

If your rejection is due to:

- Missing follower or view thresholds  
- Recent content under 1 minute  
- Recent guidelines violation  

It’s usually faster to fix the issue and reapply after the next 30-day window.
